# Playbook — planning: Compass · Two Gears · Tight Loops

**The trap:** "research → plan → build" fails not because of the *order* but because it's *linear* —
one pass, no feedback, and by the time you learn something you've already committed. Replace the
pipeline with a small always-on system.

## The four parts

- **🧭 Compass (always on).** Every cycle must plausibly move *one* measurable north-star outcome. Its
  main job is to say **NO** — kill low-leverage ideas before they cost anything. *Guards against:*
  building neat things nobody's outcome needs.
- **🔭 Explore gear (diverge).** Regular, time-boxed, *undirected* wandering — use your own product like
  a real user, read adjacent fields, log anomalies and ideas in a **spark log**. Not tied to any current
  task. This builds the "prepared mind" that lets you notice the thing worth doing. *Guards against:*
  only ever grinding the known backlog while blind spots pile up.
- **⚙️ Execute gear (converge).** For a chosen bet: **plan** (measurable "Done" + what-not-to-break) →
  **directed research** of the plan's open questions (real sources, never memory) → **build** (small
  batches, test-first) → **verify your own work**. The plan is a *living document* — research can send
  you back to replan or kill the bet.
- **🔁 Tight loops + a stop-rule (the real lever).** Small batches; verify every step so errors surface
  in minutes, not after they compound. The **stop-rule:** kill a bet the moment the evidence says it
  isn't paying off — honest updating, immune to sunk cost. *This* is how mistakes don't pile up — not
  periodic from-scratch rebuilds.

## How they connect
Explore's spark log → triaged into a prioritized backlog → becomes an Execute bet → execution surfaces
new sparks. One system, two gears feeding each other.

## Anti-patterns
- **Big-bang rewrite.** Rebuilding from scratch re-introduces already-solved problems (the "second-
  system effect"). Evolve; don't restart.
- **Fixed order as dogma.** "Plan first" holds *within* a bet — but research can bounce you back to
  replan, and Explore runs in parallel regardless. Linearity is the enemy, not sequence.

## Use it in one line
Before starting work, ask: *Which north-star does this move, and how will I measure it? What's the
smallest batch I can verify? What evidence would make me stop?* If you can't answer the first, the
Compass is saying no.
